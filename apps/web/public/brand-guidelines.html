Brand Guidelines — v1.0

A clarity engine for law.

This document defines the visual identity, voice, and design principles for a premium legal intelligence platform. Use it as the single source of truth for all product and communication decisions.

01   Brand Positioning

What the product feels like in three seconds.

Every product decision — visual, verbal, structural — should be tested against this north star. Users should feel this before they read anything.

Feel in 3 seconds

  • This is sharp.
  • This saves time.
  • This understands complexity.
  • I trust this interface.
  • This is expensive for a reason.

Never feel like

  • Generic SaaS startup
  • Courthouse website
  • Law school project
  • Cluttered government portal
  • Crypto startup

02   Naming

Names that hold weight.

The product name should feel like a legal term of art — precise, slightly institutional, and legible across contexts. Each candidate below carries different connotations.

Greyledge

Evokes the edge between settled and uncertain law. Sophisticated without being opaque. Works well as a wordmark.

Blackletter

Direct typographic reference. Signals legal tradition and authority. Strong visual identity anchor.

Provision

Clear, functional, and immediately legible for a legal research context. Easily shortened.

Letter & Spirit

Captures the interpretive tension at the center of legal practice. More editorial in tone.

Statute

Straightforward and direct. Strong recall. Works well for SEO and enterprise positioning.

Commonlaw

Clear and trustworthy. Check availability. May be competitive with existing names in the space.

04   Color System

95% neutral.
Accents signal meaning.

The color system is built around restraint. Ink and Paper carry nearly all visual weight. Accents are reserved for semantic use only — status, conflict, hierarchy, premium context — not decoration.

Ninety-five percent of any view should be neutral — Ink, Paper, Surface, and borders. Reserve all accent and semantic colors for the five percent where they signal concrete meaning: conflict, alignment, authority, premium tier. Never use color for decoration.

05   Typography

One typeface.
No exceptions.

Geist handles everything — headlines, hero copy, wordmark, interface, body text. Geist Mono is reserved for statute citations, code, and technical references. Consistency across all type roles is what gives the product its unified, institutional feel.

07   Button System

Actions should feel deliberate.

Buttons carry authority through restraint. No rounded corners, no gradients. The primary button is as close to the product's editorial character as a UI element can get.

Ink background, Paper text. Use for the main action on any view — Compare, Search, Export. One per view maximum.

Paper background, Ink border. For supporting actions — View Source, Filter, Share. Can appear alongside Primary.

Text only, Secondary Text color. For low-emphasis actions — Cancel, Skip, Dismiss. Never competes with Primary.

Red Conflict background. Reserved for actions that mark or surface legal conflicts. Use sparingly — not for delete.

NY Law — Penal §220.65

A person is guilty of criminal sale of a controlled substance when he knowingly and unlawfully sells a narcotic drug. For purposes of this article, "narcotic drug" means any controlled substance listed in schedule II(b)(1) of the public health law.

Federal — 21 USC §812

Schedule II substances have a high potential for abuse which may lead to severe psychological or physical dependence. The term includes controlled substance analogues, as defined under §813, which NY law does not expressly incorporate.

11   Motion

Subtle. Purposeful.
Nothing playful.

Motion exists to communicate system response, not to entertain. Every animation should make the interface feel faster, not busier.

Card Hover Lift

Cards shift 1px up on hover. Communicates interactivity without distraction.

transform: translateY(-1px);
transition: 120ms ease-out;
Panel Slide

Detail panels slide in from the right. Quick — 180ms. Never bouncy.

transform: translateX(0);
transition: 180ms ease-out;
Search Fade-In

Search results fade in with slight upward movement. Staggered by 40ms per row.

opacity: 0 → 1;
transition: 200ms ease-out;

Never: bounces, springs, overshoots, pulse effects, skeleton loaders with animation. These suggest uncertainty and make the product feel less trustworthy.

The product voice is precise, intelligent, and brief. It never performs excitement. It earns trust through specificity and restraint.
