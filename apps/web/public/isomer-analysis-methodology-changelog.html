v.02
Framework rewrite — textual mismatch only
April 18, 2026 · 5:55 PM EST
View version →

This version reframes the methodology around a single principle: the confidence score measures textual mismatch between state and federal scheduling language, and nothing else. Litigation frequency was removed as an input. The weight table and context modifiers were restructured to make this principle consistent across every term.

Principles
Removed litigation frequency as a weighting input. Earlier iterations considered how often each term appeared in successful categorical approach arguments. This criterion conflated how often an argument has been made with how strong the argument actually is. The revised framework evaluates terms on their textual merit alone, using the three-component justification (chemistry, federal text, gap).
Weights
Stereoisomer lowered from 0.90 to 0.82. Under the stricter framework, stereoisomer's federal comparator is partially captured by existing optical-isomer coverage, producing a smaller gap than positional isomer. Positional isomer retained at 0.90 as the cleanest textual mismatch.
Weights
Geometric isomer lowered from 0.75 to 0.70. Adjusted to reflect class-dependent federal treatment — federal § 802(14)(C) explicitly includes geometric isomers for Schedule I hallucinogens, which closes the gap entirely for that class.
Weights
Isomers (unqualified) lowered from 0.65 to 0.60. The broadening power of unqualified isomer language depends on court interpretation of the term's scope. Lowered to reflect interpretive contingency; paired with a new scope flag that surfaces the contingency explicitly.
Weights
Derivative lowered from 0.55 to 0.45. The term has the weakest chemistry anchor of any weighted term and substantial substance-class variation in federal treatment. Context modifiers now do the primary work, with effective weights ranging from near-zero (where federal parity exists) to above the base.
Weights
Salts of isomers lowered from 0.30 to 0.25. Compound / preparation lowered from 0.15 to 0.10. Boilerplate terms reduced to reflect near-complete federal parity. Both are now grouped under "Boilerplate scheduling language" in the evidence card UI and visually de-emphasized.
Structure
Analog removed from the weighted score. Moved to the scope flag tier. The Federal Analogue Act determines analog status case-by-case at prosecution rather than through scheduling text, so there is no stable federal comparator to measure against. A textual mismatch score is methodologically incoherent for this term; a flag warranting attorney review is more honest.
Structure
Context modifiers introduced. Federal § 802(14) treats isomer terms differently for Schedule I hallucinogens than for other schedules. Rather than averaging this variation into single weights, modifiers now adjust base weights per substance class at scoring time. Attorneys see the base weight, modifier, and effective weight as separate line items in the evidence card.
Structure
Three scope flags added. Analog language, interpretive contingency, and inherited category language each surface scope conditions that cannot be cleanly quantified. Flags operate independently of the confidence score and trigger attorney review without contributing numerical weight.
v.01
Initial framework — weight table and heuristic scoring
March 2026 · internal
Not published

The initial version established the original weight table, the three-component framework (chemistry, federal text, gap), and the basic rules engine for detecting isomer language in state statutes. This version was developed iteratively and never received a stable URL; it is recorded here for completeness and to document the reasoning v.02 revised.

Structure
Established the three-component framework. Every weight justified along chemistry (what the term means scientifically), federal text (how CSA scheduling treats it), and gap (the synthesis). Remained the backbone of v.02 with minor refinements.
Weights
Initial weight table. Positional isomer 0.90, stereoisomer 0.90, optical isomer 0.75, geometric isomer 0.75, isomers (unqualified) 0.65, derivative 0.55, analog 0.50, salts of isomers 0.30, compound / preparation 0.15. Weights set based on a combination of textual strength and litigation frequency — an input later removed in v.02.
Structure
Rules engine and negation detection. The parser scans a window preceding each detected term for negation markers (except, excluding, other than, does not include, shall not include) and excludes matched terms from the weighted sum. Carried forward unchanged into v.02.
Principles
Litigation frequency as an input. The original framework included a consideration for how often each term appeared in reported categorical approach cases. This pulled weights slightly toward terms that had been historically litigated (stereoisomer, for instance) and away from terms that were textually strong but less common in the record (positional isomer). v.02 removed this input as methodologically unsound.
